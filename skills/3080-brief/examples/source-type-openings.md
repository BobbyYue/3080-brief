# Source-Type Pyramid Openings

Use these only to learn structural variation. Replace every judgment, fact, boundary, and action with source-grounded content. Do not copy labels or force a source into one of these shapes.

## Data Analysis

Lead with the non-obvious insight, then separate observation from explanation:

> The aggregate result hides a segment-level reversal that changes how the outcome should be interpreted.  
> The source supports the observed contrast; the mechanism remains a hypothesis.  
> Validate the segment boundary before using the insight as a policy rule.

## Product Plan

Lead with the plan and the dependency that determines sequence:

> The product should first establish the shared capability, then open scenario-specific features in dependency order.  
> The immediate decision is the first-phase scope; later phases depend on the source-stated readiness condition.

## Technical Design

Lead with the capability change and usable constraint, without inventing business value:

> The design moves responsibility from duplicated callers into one governed service boundary.  
> This simplifies integration for the named consumers, but rollout remains limited by the compatibility condition documented in the source.

## Current-State Inventory

Lead with the map and priority judgment rather than a recommendation formula:

> Most surfaces already share the common foundation, while the remaining gaps cluster in two distinct states.  
> The map separates what is already available, what is waiting on dependency, and what requires a current-cycle change.  
> Resolve the blocking dependency before treating the remaining items as one rollout batch.

## Risk Review

Lead with risk posture and the trigger that changes action:

> Current evidence supports continued operation inside the documented boundary, not unrestricted expansion.  
> The next action changes only if the source-defined trigger is reached; otherwise monitoring remains the appropriate response.

## What These Examples Demonstrate

- Pyramid Principle is constant; sentence shape is not.
- Some sources lead with insight, some with plan, capability, map, or risk posture.
- Evidence and action appear only when they are needed to trust or use the top judgment.
- “Proceed with protection” is not a universal ending.
